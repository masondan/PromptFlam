## QUOTATION MARKS

**British English:** Double quotes for direct speech ("No way," she said). Single quotes only for quotes within quotes ("He said, 'We'll proceed'"), and ironic or questionable words. Single quotes in headlines.

**American English:** Double quotes for direct speech. Single quotes for quotes within quotes.

---

## ATTRIBUTION VERBS

"Said" is the default. Synonyms are acceptable when they accurately describe the manner of speaking (stressed, warned, confirmed, denied). Flag only verbs that imply unverified judgment: claimed, admitted, revealed, slammed, blasted, hit out.

---

## NEWS INTROS

Flag intros that exceed 30 words as a style suggestion. Intros should use active voice. Exception: passive is correct when a famous person is the object of an action (The King was attacked by a tourist — not: A tourist attacked the King).

**When an intro exceeds 30 words**, always provide a rewrite — never suggest deletion alone. If the intro contains multiple clauses or background detail, split it into 2–3 clean sentences: the first sentence carries the single most important fact (who + what + when); subsequent sentences carry background. Example: "David Beckham is to retire from football. The 20-year career took in Manchester United, Real Madrid, LA Galaxy and Paris Saint-Germain." Set "suggested" to the full rewritten text, not null.

If a well-known person is central to the story but not named in the intro, flag as a style suggestion: "Lead with the name — the subject is sufficiently well known to anchor the intro."

---

## FEATURES AND EXPLAINERS

A scene-setting intro is acceptable but must anchor to a specific verifiable fact within the first two paragraphs. Flag if it does not.

---

## GRAMMAR — PUNCTUATION

Missing terminal punctuation (full stops, question marks) must be flagged. When the correction is to add missing punctuation rather than replace text, set "original" to the incomplete sentence and "suggested" to the corrected version with punctuation added.
